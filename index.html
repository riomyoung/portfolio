Projects

Language Learning App

Learning a new language is far from simple, especially when it comes to learning how to converse. Many apps nowadays focus primarily on writing and reading and finding a native speaker to sit down with you to practice is difficult. This is why in my application, the primary focus is for the user to practice their speaking and listening skills. I have done extensive research on the quickest ways for a person to learn a language. For example, according to the study Success and failure in teaching the [r]-[l] contrast to Japanese adults, instant, repetitive feedback leads to the most robust improvements in learning, especially when it comes to pronunciation. Knowing this, I designed each interaction such that each time a user speaks, their pronunciation will be corrected so they won't accidentally memorize the wrong pronunciation and can quickly improve!

And in Students Learn Language via a Civilization Course—A Comparison of Second Language Classroom Environments, researchers learned that students enrolled in a class for in contemporary French civilization and culture that was taught in French (where the form of the language never discussed) had far higher scores in the M LA-Cooperative French Tests than students who had taken a class focused on the French language itself. With this in mind, the app is essentially a game which happens to be in another language. In it, you can earn money, learn the story of the town you moved into and complete favors for townspeople in exchange for items or to improve your relationship with those people! However, some characters might try to mislead or scam you, putting your language speaking skills to the test!

Sports Betting Bot

Arbitrage betting is done by placing multiple wagers on different outcomes of a single sports event, allowing the better to make a profit regardless of the outcome. This sports betting bot, developed by my brother, a friend and I, takes advantage of this concept in order to place the best bets on sports teams. The bot develops a formula to optimize hedge, uses formula to determine optimal hedge and uses TheOdds API to find arbitrage. Currently, using the odds given by the Odds API is our naive approach, so we are considering developing an algorithm for elo and incorporating it into the betting algorithm or tracking individual players/teams or trends in the plays that could indicate future gameplay.
